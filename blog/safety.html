Crypto Safety 101

Entering the crypto world is exciting — but also risky. Scammers are everywhere. This guide will help you protect yourself, your wallets, and your assets.

1. Always Verify Links

Many scams use fake websites that look like the real ones. Always double-check URLs before connecting your wallet.

2. Protect Your Seed Phrase

Never share your recovery phrase with anyone. Store it offline, preferably written down and kept safe.

3. Use Hardware Wallets

For long-term holding, hardware wallets (like Ledger or Trezor) are the safest option.

4. Beware of Free Tokens

Some "airdrops" are actually traps. Don’t interact with suspicious tokens in your wallet.

5. Enable 2FA

On exchanges and accounts, always use Two-Factor Authentication (Google Authenticator, Authy).
